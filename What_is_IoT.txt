What is IoT (Internet of Things)?

IoT (Internet of Things) is a network of physical devices that are
connected to the internet so they can collect, share, and exchange data
without requiring constant human intervention.

These devices contain sensors, software, and communication technologies
that allow them to interact with other devices or cloud systems.

Simple Definition The Internet of Things (IoT) is a technology that
connects everyday objects to the internet, enabling them to collect
data, communicate, and perform tasks automatically.

How IoT Works 1. Sensors collect data (temperature, motion, humidity,
etc.). 2. Internet connection (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, 4G/5G, etc.) sends the
data. 3. Cloud or server stores and processes the data. 4. Application
displays the information or sends commands back to the device. 5. The
device acts based on the received instructions.

Components of IoT - Devices/Sensors – Collect information. -
Connectivity – Transfers data over the internet. - Cloud Platform –
Stores and analyzes data. - Application/User Interface – Lets users
monitor and control devices.

Examples of IoT - Smart Home: Smart lights, smart fans, smart locks,
smart thermostats. - Healthcare: Smartwatches that monitor heart rate
and oxygen levels. - Agriculture: Soil moisture sensors for automatic
irrigation. - Transportation: GPS tracking and smart traffic
management. - Industry: Machines that monitor their own performance and
predict failures.

Advantages - Automation of everyday tasks. - Real-time monitoring. -
Saves time and energy. - Improves efficiency and productivity. - Better
decision-making using collected data.

Disadvantages - Security and privacy risks. - Dependence on internet
connectivity. - High setup cost. - Compatibility issues between
devices. - Large amounts of data to manage.

Real-Life Example A smart home system: - A temperature sensor detects
that the room is too warm. - It sends the data to the cloud. - The cloud
processes the information. - The smart air conditioner turns on
automatically. - You can also monitor and control it using your
smartphone.

Applications of IoT - Smart homes - Smart cities - Healthcare -
Agriculture - Manufacturing (Industry 4.0) - Transportation and
logistics - Environmental monitoring - Retail

One-Line Interview Answer IoT (Internet of Things) is a network of
internet-connected physical devices that collect, share, and process
data to automate tasks and improve efficiency.
